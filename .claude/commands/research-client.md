## Client Research Skill

Deep-research a client and their entire business ecosystem. This is NOT a quick scan — keep searching until you can confidently explain their business to a stranger in a pub.

**RULE: Do not stop researching until you pass the Completeness Check at the bottom. If any answer is "no" or "not sure", keep searching.**

### Input
User provides: name, LinkedIn URL, website URL

---

### Part 1: The Person

Search for the client across multiple sources. Don't stop at LinkedIn.

**LinkedIn:**
- WebSearch: "{name} LinkedIn"
- WebSearch: "{name} {company name}"
- Role, title, how long they've been there
- Previous roles — where did they come from? What's their career arc?
- Education — what level, what subject, what school?
- Posts — what do they talk about? What tone? How often?
- Recommendations — what do people say about them?
- Connections/followers — how networked are they?

**Beyond LinkedIn:**
- WebSearch: "{name}" — talks, podcasts, publications, quotes in articles
- WebSearch: "{name} {industry}" — are they known in their field?
- WebSearch: "{name} speaker" OR "{name} conference" OR "{name} interview"
- WebSearch: "{name} {company} award" OR "{name} {company} news"
- Do they have a personal website or blog?
- Have they been quoted in trade publications?
- Are they on Twitter/X? What do they post about?

**What you're building:** A mental model of this person. Not just their CV — their personality, what they care about, how they see themselves, what would impress them vs what would put them off.

### Part 2: The Company

**Basic facts:**
- WebSearch: "{company name}" — what comes up? How visible are they?
- WebSearch: "{company name} {location}"
- WebSearch: "site:{company website}" — how many pages does Google index?
- Check Companies House (UK): WebSearch: "{company name} companies house" — filing history, company age, directors, SIC codes
- Check for any registered trademarks, certifications, framework memberships

**What they actually do:**
- Read EVERY page on their website (use Chrome browser tools or WebFetch)
- What services do they list? How do they describe them?
- What jargon/language do they use? (this is critical for the pitch — speak their language)
- What's their value proposition? How do they position themselves?
- Do they specialize or generalize?

**Their clients and projects:**
- WebSearch: "{company name} case study" OR "{company name} project"
- WebSearch: "{company name} client" OR "{company name} partner"
- Look for their work mentioned in government reports, client websites, news articles
- WebSearch: "{company name} tender" OR "{company name} contract" OR "{company name} appointed"
- Check if they appear on any framework supplier lists (ESPO, Crown Commercial, G-Cloud, etc.)
- What types of organizations hire them? (councils, universities, NHS trusts, private companies)

**Revenue and scale:**
- Companies House filings — annual revenue, number of employees, profit/loss
- WebSearch: "{company name} revenue" OR "{company name} turnover"
- How many people work there? (LinkedIn company page, website team page, Companies House)
- WebSearch: "{company name} jobs" OR "{company name} hiring" — are they growing?
- What's a typical project worth? (search for tender values, published contract awards)
- WebSearch: "{industry} average project value UK" — if you can't find their specific numbers

**Their reputation:**
- WebSearch: "{company name} reviews"
- WebSearch: "{company name} testimonials"
- Google Maps listing — do they have one? Reviews?
- Any mentions on Trustpilot, Clutch, Google Reviews, industry directories
- Are they members of any industry bodies? Accreditations?

### Part 3: The Industry

**Market understanding:**
- WebSearch: "{industry} UK market size"
- WebSearch: "{industry} trends 2025 2026"
- WebSearch: "how do {their clients} find {their service type}"
- WebSearch: "{industry} buyer journey" — do people Google first? Get referrals? Use frameworks?
- What's driving demand in this sector? (policy changes, funding cycles, regulations)
- Is the market growing or shrinking?

**How their clients buy:**
- This is CRITICAL for the pitch. You need to know the decision-making process.
- Public sector: tenders, frameworks, approved supplier lists, procurement rules
- Private sector: referrals, Google search, industry events, directories
- WebSearch: "{their service type} procurement process"
- WebSearch: "{their client type} how to find {their service type}"
- What does a procurement officer or hiring manager actually DO when they need this service?

### Part 4: Competitors (DEEP DIVE)

This is where you build the "you're behind" evidence. Be thorough.

**Find competitors:**
- WebSearch: "{their service} {their location}" — who appears above them?
- WebSearch: "{their service} UK" — who are the national players?
- WebSearch: "{their exact service niche}" — who specializes in the same thing?
- Check their LinkedIn: look at "People also viewed" companies
- Check industry directories and framework supplier lists
- WebSearch: "best {their service type} companies UK" OR "{their service type} firms"
- Aim for 5-8 competitors, mix of local and national

**For EACH competitor, document:**

| What | How to find it |
|------|---------------|
| Website URL | Google search |
| Design quality (1-10) | Visit their site — is it modern, professional, custom-built or template? |
| Content depth | Do they have detailed case studies? Blog? Resources? Whitepapers? |
| SEO presence | Do they rank above the client for key terms? How many pages indexed? |
| Blog/thought leadership | Do they publish? How often? What topics? |
| Case studies | How detailed? Do they show data, outcomes, testimonials? |
| Lead capture | Chatbot? Newsletter? Downloadable resources? Contact form quality? |
| Social proof | Client logos, testimonials, awards, certifications visible? |
| Team visibility | Do they show their team? Bios? Photos? |
| Chatbot | Do they have one? What does it do? |
| Overall impression | If you were a potential client comparing, who would you choose and why? |

**The key question:** If a potential client Googles "{their service} {their location}" and sees 5 results, where does our client rank in terms of web presence? Usually dead last — and that's the pitch.

Save competitor analysis to `clients/{slug}/content/competitor-analysis.md`

### Part 5: Search Visibility Reality Check

**Actually test their visibility:**
- WebSearch: "{their core service} {their city}" — do they appear?
- WebSearch: "{their core service} {their region}" — do they appear?
- WebSearch: "{their core service} UK" — do they appear?
- WebSearch: "{specific service they offer}" — do they appear?
- Try 5-10 search terms a potential client would actually use

**Document for each search:**
| Search Term | Client Appears? | Who Does Appear? (Top 3-5) |
|------------|----------------|---------------------------|

This becomes hard evidence: "We searched for '{service} {location}' and you don't appear. Here's who does."

### Part 6: Social & Content Presence

- LinkedIn company page — followers, posting frequency, engagement
- Twitter/X — do they exist? Active?
- YouTube — any videos?
- Published articles, whitepapers, reports
- Mentions in press/media
- Speaking engagements, webinars
- Compare to competitors: who's more visible online?

---

### Completeness Check

**DO NOT move to the next step until ALL of these are "yes":**

- [ ] I can explain what this person does and what motivates them in 3 sentences
- [ ] I can explain how their company makes money in 1 sentence
- [ ] I know who their actual clients are (not vague — specific types of organizations)
- [ ] I know how those clients find and choose providers like them
- [ ] I know what a typical project/contract is worth (exact or estimated range with source)
- [ ] I have at least 5 competitors identified with their websites visited
- [ ] I can rank those competitors by web presence quality (and explain why)
- [ ] I've tested 5+ real search terms and documented whether the client appears
- [ ] I know what's on EVERY page of the client's current website
- [ ] I understand the client's industry well enough to use their jargon correctly
- [ ] I have enough competitor evidence to show the client they're behind
- [ ] I have enough business data to frame ROI realistically (not made-up numbers)

**If any answer is "no" — keep researching. Do more searches. Read more pages. The pitch quality is directly proportional to research depth.**

- `clients/{slug}/content/competitor-analysis.md` — detailed competitor breakdown
